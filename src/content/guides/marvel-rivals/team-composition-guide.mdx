Team composition in Marvel Rivals decides fights before aim diff shows up. Stack roles wrong and even strong players lose objective control.

## Default 6v6 template

| Slot | Role | Job |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1–2 | Vanguard | Space, peel, engage |
| 3–4 | Duelist | Damage, picks, flank |
| 5–6 | Strategist | Heals, cleanses, buffs |

## When to flex

- **Double Strategist** — against poke-heavy comps or when teammates feed on fragile Duelists
- **Triple Vanguard** — rare, but works on tight objective maps with environmental kills
- **Double flank Duelist** — only with comms and peel coverage

## Ultimate coordination

1. Call ult before engaging — voice or ping
2. Layer crowd control before damage ults
3. Save defensive ults for enemy combo windows
4. Do not blow four ults into one overextended target

## Map-aware comps

| Map type | Adjustment |
| --- | --- |
| Open lanes | More hitscan Duelists |
| Vertical maps | Mobile heroes + ranged support |
| Objective choke | Area denial Vanguards |

## Common comp mistakes

- Four Duelists, zero sustain
- No Vanguard on payload-style modes
- Ignoring enemy counter-picks between rounds

## Bottom line

Run 2-2-2 as your baseline, flex Strategist up when needed, and coordinate ultimates. Composition wins more ranked games than solo hero mastery.
