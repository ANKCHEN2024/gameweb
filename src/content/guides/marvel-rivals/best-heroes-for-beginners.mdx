Marvel Rivals drops you into 6v6 hero combat with iconic characters. New players should start with heroes that teach fundamentals without punishing mistakes harshly.

## Best beginner heroes by role

| Role | Hero picks | Why |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Vanguard | Captain America, Groot | Clear peel and space control |
| Duelist | Spider-Man, Punisher | Simple damage patterns |
| Strategist | Mantis, Luna Snow | Straightforward heals and buffs |

## Vanguard starters

**Captain America** teaches shield timing and frontline positioning. You absorb pressure while your team shoots — perfect for learning map control.

**Groot** offers area denial with clearer visual cues than faster vanguards. Slower pace helps new players read fights.

## Duelist starters

**Punisher** is a grounded hitscan-style pick with readable cooldowns. Good for learning aim fundamentals without aerial chaos.

**Spider-Man** is mobile but popular — practice web mobility in custom modes before ranked.

## Strategist starters

**Mantis** provides heals with obvious targeting. You learn positioning by staying near allies without hard flick requirements.

**Luna Snow** mixes damage and support — flexible when teams lack coordination.

## Heroes to delay until fundamentals click

- High mobility assassins with complex escape chains
- Heroes requiring precise team combo timing every fight
- Niche picks countered hard in current meta

## First-week learning plan

1. Play 10 quick play matches on one Vanguard
2. Add one Duelist for damage role queue
3. Learn one Strategist for when support is needed
4. Watch kill cams to understand ultimate timing

## Bottom line

Start with Captain America or Groot, Punisher or Spider-Man, and Mantis or Luna Snow. One hero per role beats mastering a single flashy pick.
