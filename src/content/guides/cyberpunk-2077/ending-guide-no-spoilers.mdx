This guide explains how endings work without ruining plot twists. Think of it as a decision framework.

## How endings are gated

Endings in Cyberpunk 2077 depend on:

1. **Main mission progress** — how far the central conflict has escalated
2. **Key ally quest completion** — major companions need their arcs advanced
3. **Final choice structure** — dialogue and mission selections at the point of no return

## Point of no return

- Late main story triggers a warning before irreversible missions
- **Manual save** before accepting final chain missions
- Finish side gigs you care about before entering endgame sequence

## Keeping options open (no spoiler checklist)

| Action | Reason |
| --- | --- |
| Complete major companion arcs | Unlocks relationship-dependent branches |
| Respond consistently in key dialogues | Some flags are cumulative |
| Do not skip Johnny-related missions | Central to multiple outcomes |
| Own Phantom Liberty content | Adds additional decision weight |

## Phantom Liberty interaction

If you own the expansion, complete its core arc before judging your ending options. It can alter epilogue context without replacing base structure entirely.

## Replay advice

- One playthrough cannot see everything naturally
- Use post-ending character reset or new playthrough for alt branches
- Wiki decision trees help after your first run

## Bottom line

Save before the endgame warning, finish major companion quests, and expect multiple valid endings — not one "correct" route.
