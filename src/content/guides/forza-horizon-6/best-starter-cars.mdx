Your first cars in Forza Horizon 6 shape how fast you earn credits and complete story chapters. Pick versatile platforms before collecting supercars.

## Starter car criteria

| Trait | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| A-class ready | Matches early events |
| Good grip | Forgiving in rain |
| Upgrade potential | Grows with credits |
| Balanced stats | Works on road and dirt |

## Recommended starter categories

### Hot hatches (A class)

- Excellent handling for PR stunts
- Cheap upgrades
- Strong in tight festival routes

### Japanese sports coupes

- Balanced power-to-weight
- Large aftermarket upgrade tree
- Popular in seasonal playlists

### European touring cars

- Stable at high speed
- Good for highway showcase events
- Strong dirt conversion options

## Cars to avoid early

- Top-tier hypercars before you can tune them
- Heavy SUVs for circuit races unless event-specific
- Duplicate low-PI builds cluttering garage

## Upgrade path

1. Race stock car in first chapter
2. Buy grip and power upgrades incrementally
3. Tune gearing before maxing engine
4. Save credits for one S-class goal car mid-game

## Bottom line

Start with a tunable A-class hot hatch or sports coupe. Master handling and upgrade paths before buying expensive supercars you cannot tune yet.
